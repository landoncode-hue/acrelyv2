# Core UX Principles & Guidelines

This document outlines the foundational UX principles, frameworks, and guidelines that govern the design and development of Acrely. Adhering to these standards ensures a high-quality, user-centered product that builds trust and drives adoption.

## 1. User-Centered Design (UCD)
We prioritize user needs, behaviors, and goals throughout the entire development lifecycle. Every feature should start with an understanding of who the user is and what problem we are solving for them.

## 2. Consistency & Standards
We use familiar patterns, colors, fonts, and interactions across all platforms (Web, iOS, and Android).
- **Goal**: Reduce cognitive load by making the interface predictable.
- **Action**: Follow established design system tokens and platform-specific guidelines (Apple HIG / Google Material Design).

## 3. Feedback
The system should always provide clear and immediate responses to user actions.
- **Examples**: "Saved" toast notifications, loading indicators for asynchronous tasks, and haptic feedback on mobile.

## 4. Error Prevention & Recovery
Design to minimize mistakes before they happen. When errors do occur, offer clear "emergency exits."
- **Action**: Use confirmation dialogs for destructive actions, provide helpful error messages, and support undo/redo where appropriate.

## 5. Usability & Efficiency
Tasks should be easy, fast, and relevant.
- **Goal**: Minimize the number of steps required to complete common workflows.
- **Action**: Use sensible defaults and progressive disclosure to hide complexity.

## 6. Accessibility (a11y)
Ensure the software is usable by people with diverse abilities, adhering to **WCAG standards**.
- **Focus**: Color contrast, screen reader compatibility, and keyboard navigation.

## 7. Simplicity & Clarity
Avoid clutter and focus on essential information.
- **Action**: Use strong visual hierarchy to guide the user's eye to the most important actions.

# Key Frameworks & Laws

### Nielsen Norman Group Heuristics
We use the 10 usability heuristics as a checklist for UI audits, specifically:
- **Visibility of System Status**
- **Match Between System & Real World**
- **User Control and Freedom**

### Laws of UX
- **Fitts's Law**: The time to acquire a target is a function of the distance to and size of the target. (Make buttons large and easy to hit).
- **Aesthetic-Usability Effect**: Users often perceive aesthetically pleasing design as design that’s more usable.

### Platform Guidelines
- **Apple Human Interface Guidelines (HIG)**: For iOS-specific behaviors.
- **Google Material Design**: For Android-specific behaviors.

### ISO Standards
- **ISO/IEC 25010**: Incorporating usability as a key software quality characteristic.

# How UX Connects to Software Quality

- **Reduces Frustration**: Good UX minimizes perceived bugs and performance issues.
- **Builds Trust**: Professional aesthetics and reliable interactions build brand credibility.
- **Drives Adoption**: Efficient software gets used; poor UX leads to abandonment.
- **Informs Development**: These standards guide requirements gathering, modeling, iterative design, and testing, ensuring we meet real-world needs.
